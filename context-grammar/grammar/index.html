8 Tokens That Teach AI
to Read the Room

Every waiter reads the room before speaking. Context Grammar gives AI the same instinct — through 8 measurable dimensions of human state.

Why Tokens?

The Waiter Who Notices

A great waiter doesn't just take orders. They notice you're rushing. They see your boss is at the table. They remember you were here last Tuesday. They adjust everything — tone, timing, recommendations — without being asked.

AI today is the waiter who recites the daily specials to someone crying at the table.

It knows what you asked for. It has no idea what's happening around you. Context Tokens fix that. They give AI 8 dimensions to read the room — six that describe your situation, two that control the relationship.

Part 1 — The 6 Situation Tokens
Situation Tokens

Six Sensors for What's Happening

These tokens describe the world around the user. They're inputs — raw signals that AI reads, not settings the user controls.

①
Physical State "Are they rushing or relaxed?"
Restaurant Customer rushes in, coat half-off, scanning for a seat. The waiter brings water before the menu. A couple strolls in on a Saturday evening. Full welcome, wine list, no rush. Same restaurant. Different service. The waiter read the body, not the booking.
Technology Reality Activity Recognition API detects walking, running, driving, still. Galaxy Watch adds heart rate, stress index, sleep debt. Accelerometer data is cheap and reliable. The gap: combining these signals into a single "state" that drives design decisions.
★★★★☆ Mostly shipping
②
Cognitive Load "How much mental bandwidth remains?"
Restaurant Two people deep in conversation, leaning in, hands moving. The waiter watches from a distance. A solo diner staring at the ceiling, looking around. The waiter approaches. Reading cognitive load is reading the moment between "I'm available" and "don't interrupt me."
Technology Reality This is estimation only. Time of day, calendar density, recent activity patterns, number of app switches in the last 10 minutes. No brain scanner. No EEG. The model infers bandwidth from observable proxies — and it will be wrong sometimes. Honesty matters here.
★★☆☆☆ Speculative — estimation only
Honest Design Note

Cognitive Load is the most speculative token. There is no direct measurement today. Context Grammar uses it as an estimation layer, not a certainty. Design decisions based on this token should always include graceful fallbacks.

③
Social Exposure "Who else is watching?"
Restaurant Date night. The waiter dims the energy. Quiet suggestions. No mention of the loyalty card discount. Business lunch with the CEO. No casual banter. Precise timing. Who's at the table changes everything about how service is delivered.
Technology Reality Proximity sensors detect nearby devices. Voice ID distinguishes family members. Samsung's Family Hub already supports up to 6 member profiles. Visual detection (on-device) can estimate crowd density. The gap: a unified protocol that tells AI "you're in public" vs "you're alone" across all devices.
★★★★☆ Mostly shipping
④
Priority Weight "When demands collide, what wins?"
Restaurant A child has a nut allergy. The meal must taste great. The bill should stay reasonable. When these three demands collide, allergy wins. Always. A great waiter never needs to be told this hierarchy. They know: safety beats taste beats price.
Technology Reality Calendar priority levels, notification settings, Gmail Priority Inbox — pieces of the puzzle exist. The gap: cross-domain priority that resolves "your meeting starts in 5 minutes but your daughter's school is calling" without asking the user to build rules manually.
★★★☆☆ Partially shipping
Design Note

Priority Weight has two layers: current urgency (this token) and learned tradeoff patterns (stored in the Brain). The token captures what's pressing right now. The Brain remembers how you've resolved similar collisions before.

⑤
Form Factor "What screens surround them?"
Restaurant Counter seat — one dish at a time, quick bites, minimal conversation. Private dining room — full course presentation, multiple plates, space to linger. The physical space shapes the service. The screen shapes the experience.
Technology Reality Device type detection is trivial. Samsung DeX, Apple CarPlay, Continuity Handoff — all production-ready. The gap: dynamic multi-screen allocation. Knowing "the TV is on and you're holding your phone" should change what appears on each screen simultaneously.
★★★★☆ Mostly shipping
⑥
Feasibility "What's actually possible right now?"
Restaurant "That fish is sold out tonight." A bad waiter stops there. A great waiter says: "But the sea bass came in this morning, and the chef does it the same way." Feasibility isn't about saying no. It's about proposing what's possible instead.
Technology Reality Inventory APIs, weather APIs, real-time delivery estimates, store hours — individual feasibility checks are mature. The gap: multi-constraint optimization. Combining "it's raining + the store closes in 20 minutes + you have a toddler in the car" into a single feasibility score.
★★★☆☆ Partially shipping
Part 2 — The 2 Relationship Dials
Relationship Dials

Two Controls for How Far AI Can Go

Situation Tokens describe what's happening. Relationship Dials control what AI is allowed to do about it. They're not sensors — they're boundaries.

⑦
Autonomy Dial "How much can AI act on its own?"
Stage 1
Suggest
Stage 2
Confirm
Stage 3
Notify
Stage 4
Auto
Restaurant First visit. "Here's the menu. Take your time." Third visit. "The usual?" Twentieth visit. The food just appears. The progression from full menu to automatic is trust earned through consistency. Not a switch you flip on day one.
Technology Reality Claude Code uses 3-stage autonomy modes. Cursor IDE has an autonomy slider. Agent frameworks ship with suggest/confirm/auto patterns as standard. The gap: consumer-facing gradual dial that adjusts per-domain, not a binary on/off.
★★★☆☆ Shipping in dev tools
Key Insight

The Autonomy Dial is not purely user-controlled. Three forces shape it: the service's default setting (Netflix starts high), AI adjustment based on observed trust patterns, and the user's active choice. Your Uber Eats doesn't ask permission to reorder — because the service default already assumed autonomy.

⑧
Disclosure Dial "How much does AI know about you?"
Level 1
Full
Level 2
Summary
Level 3
Existence
Level 4
Hidden
Restaurant Tell the waiter your allergies, your anniversary, your wine preference. You get a perfect evening. Tell them nothing. You get the generic menu and a "is everything okay?" every ten minutes. The more you share, the better the service. But sharing is always your choice.
Technology Reality App-level permissions exist. Gemini Connected Apps lets you choose which services share data. Samsung privacy dashboard shows what's accessed. The gap: a unified cross-domain dial. "Full disclosure to my health AI, summary only for shopping, hidden from social media" — one interface, granular control.
★☆☆☆☆ Concept stage
Key Insight

Disclosure is the prerequisite for Autonomy. You cannot say "don't tell AI anything about me" and also expect "but handle everything automatically." That combination is logically dangerous. The Disclosure Dial must open before the Autonomy Dial can advance.

Bridge
Rule Engine — Translating Context into Action
Rule Engine

8 tokens go in.
Specific design rules come out.

The Rule Engine transforms the token/brain combination into concrete behavioral rules. Not recommendations — rules. If/then logic at scale, running silently on every interaction.

if Physical State = driving → UI = audio only
if Social Exposure = boss_nearby → hide personal notifications
if Autonomy = Auto AND price < ¥500 → purchase without confirmation
if Cognitive Load = high → reduce options to top 3
if Autonomy = Auto AND price > ¥15,000 → always confirm (Dynamic Friction)
if Disclosure = hidden AND Autonomy > Confirm → constraint violation → reset Autonomy

The restaurant waiter's intuition — "this table needs minimal interruption," "the regular wants their usual," "check before suggesting the spicy dish" — expressed as computable if/then rules.

The Core Formula

From Signals to Design Rules

Eight tokens. One engine. Infinite combinations. Here's how they connect.

6 Situation Tokens
"Here's what's happening"
+
2 Relationship Dials
"Here's how far AI can go"
=
Rule Engine
Generates design rules

The six Situation Tokens declare context. The two Dials set boundaries. The Rule Engine takes both and generates specific design decisions — what to show, when to interrupt, how much detail to include, which device to use.

No single token works alone. A rushing user (Physical State) with low cognitive bandwidth (Cognitive Load) at a business dinner (Social Exposure) on a watch (Form Factor) gets a fundamentally different experience than the same person, relaxed at home, on a TV. Same person. Same AI. Different grammar.
