Grammar — Easy Mode

What does AI need to read the room?

A great waiter notices 8 things before they say a word. AI today notices almost none of them. Let's introduce them, one at a time.

Step 1 — Feel it first

Same person. Same phone. Two different days.

Look at what AI does today (left) versus what it could do (right). The difference isn't more features. It's something simpler.

AI today

"Here are your 12 notifications."

You're driving. Phone buzzes. "Your mom liked your photo." "15% off shoes today only!" "Time to log your steps." Same notifications you'd get if you were on the couch.

With Context Grammar

"Reading aloud: one urgent message from your daughter's school."

Same phone. Same data. But AI noticed: you're driving, your daughter has a school day, everything else can wait. So it spoke instead of buzzed, and only about what mattered.

AI got smarter not because it learned more. It got smarter because it read the situation. That's all Context Grammar is — giving AI the words to describe a situation.

Step 2 — One thing at a time

The first thing AI needs to read: your body.

Reading #1 — Physical State

Are you running, driving, or sitting still?

That's it. That's the first thing. AI needs to know if you're moving, and how.

It sounds obvious. But almost no AI today actually adjusts for it.

In a restaurant

A customer rushes in with their coat half-off, scanning for a seat. The waiter brings water before the menu. A couple strolls in on a Saturday night — full welcome, wine list, no rush. Same restaurant. Same waiter. Different service. The waiter read the body, not the booking.

On your phone

If you're walking → notifications appear at eye level. If you're driving → AI reads them aloud. If you're sitting at your desk → AI shows the full message with images. Same phone. Same data. The phone read your body.

That's one reading. Just from your body, AI can already act differently. Now let's add a second.

3 readings

Add: how busy your mind is, and who is around you.

Two more readings. Each one solves a real situation that today's AI gets wrong every day.

Reading #1

Your body

Running, driving, sitting still — already covered above.

Reading #2

Your mental bandwidth

Are you focused on a hard task, or relaxing? AI can guess from your calendar, the time, and how often you switch apps.

Reading #3

Who else is around

Are you alone, with family, or with your boss in the room? AI can detect this from voice, nearby devices, or the calendar event you're in.

With just 3 readings, AI already knows enough to act differently.

"Driving + low mental bandwidth + alone" → read aloud only urgent messages. "Sitting + focused + boss in the room" → silence everything except the meeting alarm. Three readings, two completely different behaviors.

6 readings

Three more readings, and AI can read almost any moment.

You don't need to remember these names. Just notice that each one solves a real, everyday problem.

Reading #1

Your body

Are you moving, and how? (Walking, driving, sitting…)

Reading #2

Your mental bandwidth

How much room is left in your head for decisions?

Reading #3

Who is around

Alone? Family? Boss? Strangers in public?

Reading #4

What matters most right now

If two things compete for your attention — the work email vs. the school nurse calling — which wins? AI needs to know.

Reading #5

What screen you're using

Watch? Phone? TV? Car dashboard? The same message has to look completely different on each.

Reading #6

What's actually possible right now

Is the store open? Is the item in stock? Is it raining? AI shouldn't suggest what can't happen.

These 6 readings describe almost any moment of your day.

Combined, they tell AI everything it needs to know about your situation. But there's still one thing missing — and it's important.

A natural question

"But what if I don't want AI doing things for me without permission?"

That's exactly the right question. The 6 readings let AI understand your situation. But understanding isn't permission to act. So Context Grammar adds 2 more — not readings, but controls. Dials that you turn.

2 dials (different from the 6 readings)

Two dials you control. They decide how far AI can go.

Reading is automatic. AI does it on its own. But these two dials? You decide where they sit. (And so do the services you use, and so does AI based on its track record. We'll explain that part later.)

Dial 1

Autonomy Dial

"How much can AI do on its own?"

Suggest
Confirm
Notify
Auto

Like a restaurant regular. First visit: "Here's the menu, take your time" (Suggest). Third visit: "The usual?" (Confirm). Twentieth visit: the food just appears (Auto). The dial moves slowly, with trust earned over time.

Dial 2

Disclosure Dial

"How much do you let AI know about you?"

Full
Summary
Existence
Hidden

Like telling a waiter about your allergies. Tell them everything — the perfect evening. Tell them nothing — the generic menu. The more you share, the better the service. But sharing is always your choice.

A simple rule connects them: you can't say "tell AI nothing" AND "let AI handle everything."

If the Disclosure Dial is at "Hidden," the Autonomy Dial can't be at "Auto." That combination is logically dangerous — AI would be acting on guesses about a person it knows nothing about. Disclosure must come before Autonomy. Always.

The official name for these is "8 Context Tokens" — but you don't need the name to understand them. You just saw all 8, one by one, and you understand what each does. That's the point.

A real example

"You wanted yogurt, but the store is out. Now what?"

This is when all 8 tokens work together. Your answer to this question is called your Substitution Mode. There are four of them.

EXACT

"Only what I asked for." No Meiji Bulgarian yogurt? Then no yogurt today. Use this for medications, allergies, brand loyalty.

FLEXIBLE

"Close enough is fine." Get any other Bulgarian-style yogurt. Use this for routine groceries.

EXPLORING

"Show me something new." What about that new Greek yogurt everyone's talking about? Use this when you're open to discovery.

SURPRISE

"Delight me." AI found an artisan yogurt from a local farm. Want to try it? Use this for gifts, food adventures, when you trust AI deeply.

Notice: Surprise mode requires high Autonomy. You can't say "surprise me" while keeping AI on a short leash — that's a contradiction. The 8 tokens work together, not separately.
