Context Grammar

A Design Language
for Agentic AI

AI can write your emails and book your flights. It still has no idea you are exhausted. Context Grammar is the missing layer — eight tokens that let AI read human state, not just human words.

The Problem

The Problem No One Named

AI assistants are brilliant at processing requests. They are blind to the person making them.

01 — Observation

AI can do incredible things

It writes code, generates images, plans trips, manages calendars. Every month the capabilities list gets longer. The technology works.

02 — Mystery

But it responds the same way every time

Whether you are relaxed or rushing. Alone or with your boss in the room. Browsing casually or making a decision that matters. Same input, same output. Why?

03 — Discovery

There is no shared language for human context

Designers say “the user is stressed.” Engineers ask “what is the data point for stressed?” Nobody has an answer. The conceptual layer is missing.

Context Grammar is that missing layer. Eight tokens that translate human state into structured signals AI can read, reason about, and act on.

Architecture

How It Works

Five stages. One pipeline. Context enters as raw signal and exits as adapted behavior.

Floor 1 Intent

What the user wants

Explicit or implicit. Sometimes the user states it directly. Sometimes the system infers it from the intersection of tokens and memory.

  • Explicit — stated directly
  • Active — inferred from action
  • Passive — inferred from patterns
  • Ambient — continuous background signal

Floor 2 Grammar

Six situation tokens. Two relationship dials.

Physical State. Cognitive Load. Social Exposure. Priority Weight. Form Factor. Feasibility. Plus two dials that govern the relationship: Autonomy Dial and Disclosure Dial. Together they describe who you are right now.

Deep dive into the 8 tokens →
Floor 3 Brain

Three layers of memory

Three layers updated at three speeds. Identity changes in years. Accumulated Learning changes in weeks. Right Now changes in seconds. Every AI today has memory; none of them organize it to act on this exact moment.

Deep dive into Brain →
Floor 4 Rule Engine

Context becomes design rules

Tokens and memory feed into a rule engine that produces concrete design decisions. What to show, what to hide, how much autonomy to grant, which modality to use.

Floor 5 Response

Delegate. Escalate. Adapt.

Every adaptive response falls into one of these three. The system handles it, asks a human, or changes its presentation. Twenty-three concrete AX patterns, three categories.

Cross-cutting Trust Design

The temporal arc across all floors

Trust isn't a single floor — it runs along the side of the entire tower. The Disclosure Dial governs what humans share. The Autonomy Dial governs what AI can do. Together they form a 6-month temporal arc from stranger to ambient trust, with Dynamic Friction capping autonomy for high-risk actions.
