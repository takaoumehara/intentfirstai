Introducing Context Grammar

The missing layer
between humans and AI.

Today's AI can process your words, but it can't sense your world. It doesn't know you're walking through a crowded station with one free hand, mentally drained after a long day, trying to make a quick decision for your family.

Context Grammar defines 8 tokens — dimensions like Intent, Physical State, Cognitive Load, and Social Exposure — that capture these invisible human states and translate them into a structured language AI can actually read. The result: AI that doesn't just respond to what you type, but adapts to who you are and what you need, right now.

Context Grammar — Brain

The memory that makes
context possible.

Tokens read your situation right now. Brain is what makes that situation meaningful. A 3-layer memory structure that knows who you are, what you've learned together, and what's happening this moment.

01
Identity

The stable foundation. Family structure, dietary restrictions, values, cultural context. This layer rarely changes — it's who you are, not what you're doing.

02
Accumulated Learning

Grows through repeated interactions. Shopping patterns, schedule rhythms, taste preferences, tradeoff habits. What a great assistant learns over months of working together.

03
Right Now

Ephemeral and real-time. Current location, active devices, recent activity. This layer is alive — it updates every minute and disappears when the moment passes.
