Context Token 8 — Disclosure Dial

Disclosure is
protection and connection

The Disclosure Dial doesn't just hide data. It actively shapes what flows between people and AI, in two distinct directions.

Protective
Connective

Protective Disclosure

Controlling what others can see about you. A contextual filter on outgoing information, applied per-person and per-domain.

Daughter's health data hidden from father
Gynecology visit records set to HIDDEN level, per-person and per-domain. Father sees no trace.
Prices hidden from children on shared TV
Social Exposure token detects children present, triggers HIDDEN for financial data on the family display.

Connective Disclosure

Actively pushing personal data to strengthen relationships. Not just allowing access, but deliberately sharing to be known.

Wife shares dance video highlights weekly
DIGEST level. AI curates her best practice clips into a weekly family recap. Sharing without effort.
Daughter pushes art posts to parents
PUSH level. New digital art auto-surfaces on parents' display. "Look what I made" without saying it.

Disclosure Levels

Each direction has its own granularity. Click a level to see what it means.

Protective Direction
HIDDEN ← EXISTENCE ← SUMMARY ← FULL
Hidden
Data exists but is completely invisible to this audience.
Existence
Audience knows the data category exists, but sees no content.
Summary
Aggregated or anonymized view. Trends, not specifics.
Full
Complete data visible to this audience. No filtering applied.
Connective Direction
AVAILABLE → DIGEST → PUSH
Available
Data is accessible if someone asks or AI pulls it. Passive sharing.
Digest
AI curates and sends periodic summaries to chosen audience.
Push
Actively surfaces content to the audience in real time.
Key Insight

Most privacy models only think about protection — what to hide.
The Disclosure Dial also models connection — what to actively share.

The same dial, two directions.
One person's HIDDEN is another person's PUSH.
