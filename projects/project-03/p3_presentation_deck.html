Translation Limits

The most honest thing an AI can do
is admit what it can't translate.

✅ AI can translate
• "Black" → main colour filter
• "Waterproof" → GORE-TEX / water-resistant
• "No fluffy" → fleece/sherpa absence (NLP)
• "PE-compatible" → gym-safe review analysis
• "Major brand" → brand filter
❌ Only humans can judge
• "White soles are ugly" → never said until seen
• "Ridged soles won't work in gym" → too specific
• "That lace colour is weird" → aesthetic
• "Logo is too big" → Social Job
• "That pattern is ugly" → taste, not data

Approval Gate

Every suggestion gets
3 possible responses.

✓
Approve
そのまま購入
AIの提案をそのまま受け入れる。サイズ・色・配送先すべてOK。
✎
Edit
インライン編集
サイズ変更、色変更、配送日指定。提案の骨格は活かしつつ調整。
✕
Reject
見送り・理由を記録
「今回はやめておく」— 理由がBrainに蓄積され、次回の提案精度が上がる。
Restaurant Metaphor
シェフが料理を出した。(1) そのまま食べる、(2)「もう少し塩を」と調整を頼む、(3)「今日はやめておきます」と返す。この3つの選択肢が常にある。 AIの提案も同じ — 承認・編集・却下、すべてが学習データになる。

Design Decisions

Why hide prices from the TV?
Marcus is 13 — if he sees £190 next to £69, he'll pick the cheap one or feel guilty. Social Job breaks when price becomes visible to children.
Rejected: showing prices on TV for "transparency"
Why admit the Translation Layer's limits?
"No white soles" was never said because Naomi didn't know it was a constraint. You can't translate what hasn't been articulated. AI + Human > AI alone.
Rejected: pretending AI can perfectly filter all constraints
Why confirm before cross-applying rejections?
Siblings share some constraints (sole colour) but not others (style). "Apply to Zara too?" respects both efficiency and individuality.
Rejected: fully automatic cross-profile application
